The features of different cells were first identified by using light microscopes. These microscopes are still in frequent use. the specimen you want to look at is places on a glass slide, it is then placed on the stage of the microscope. A light is then shone through the specimen from below, you look down through the barrel of the microscope while the light travels up through an objective lens which magnifies the image and then through an eyepiece lens which magnifies it further still.
⟩
Over time the quality of light microscopes improved allowing higher magnifications.
⟩
Then came the invention of the electron microscope which is different in a number of ways
⟩
Electron microscopes use electrons, rather than light, to visualise and magnify the specimen under observation
⟩
Very much higher magnifications became possible along with three-dimensional images, however living specimens can't be used
⟩
Studies and observations that use electron microscopes have enabled us to build, in great detail, a picture of how cellular structures are arranged in cells.
